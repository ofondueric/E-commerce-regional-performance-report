# E-commerce-regional-performance-report
An end-to-end data analytics project analyzing e-commerce sales performance across regions, identifying revenue trends, top-performing markets, customer behavior patterns, and actionable business insights using Microsoft Excel

## Project Overview

Retail businesses generate large volumes of transactional data every day, but turning that data into actionable business insights remains a challenge. This project analyzes sales performance across multiple supermarket branches to identify revenue drivers, customer purchasing behavior, product performance, and profitability trends.

The analysis was conducted using Microsoft Excel, leveraging Power Query, Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, Slicers, and Timelines to build an interactive business intelligence dashboard.

## Business Problem

Management lacked visibility into the factors driving revenue, profitability, and customer satisfaction across branches, product categories, customer segments, and sales periods.

This project aims to answer the following question:

**How can management improve revenue and profitability by understanding sales performance across branches, product lines, customer segments, and time patterns?**

## Company Background

Brajira is a retail supermarket chain operating three branches across Myanmar.

## Key Business Insights

### Branch Performance

Giza branch generated the strongest overall performance during the quarter.

Cairo branch recorded the lowest sales performance, trailing Giza by approximately 4%.

The overall decline in February 2019 sales was primarily driven by weaker performance in the Cairo branch.

Giza contributed approximately 34% of total quarterly gross income.

### Product Performance

Food & Beverages generated the highest revenue across all product categories.

Fashion & Accessories achieved the highest customer ratings.

Analysis revealed a weak negative relationship between customer ratings and sales performance, suggesting that highly rated products do not necessarily generate the highest revenue.



### Customer Behaviour

Loyalty program members contributed approximately 59% of total revenue.

Members consistently provided higher product ratings than non-members.

Customer membership appears strongly associated with increased spending and engagement.

### Time Trend Analysis

Weekend sales outperformed weekday sales throughout the quarter.

Sales activity typically increased on Fridays and Saturdays before declining slightly on Sundays.

Weekend demand patterns indicate opportunities for optimized staffing and inventory management.

## Business Recommendations

### Improve Branch Performance

Implement targeted sales promotions and marketing campaigns in Cairo to improve branch-level performance.

### Enhance Customer Feedback Quality

Replace generic rating measures with a structured star-rating system to improve customer satisfaction analysis.

### Optimize Product Portfolio

Monitor slow-moving inventory closely.

Consider supplier changes, stock reallocation, or product discontinuation where necessary.

### Increase Customer Retention

Encourage conversion of regular customers into loyalty program members through targeted incentives and membership benefits.

### Align Operations with Demand

Adjust staffing levels and inventory allocation to reflect higher weekend demand and sales activity.

## Limitations

The analysis covers only one quarter (January–March 2019).

Limited historical data restricts seasonal and long-term trend analysis.

Customer feedback data lacks sufficient detail for deeper sentiment analysis.
